Hereditary diseases can be defined as a disease that occurs in individuals as a result of inheriting genetic disorders from parents. This study aims to develop an extended version of the current national pre-marital screening programme, to maximize the productivity/properness/flourishing of individuals in the population and to guide the effective use of healthcare system resources. Evaluating the efficiency of the current national pre-marital screening programme, by examining evidence reported from previous studies about the efficiency of applying the current programme in reducing the incidence of hereditary disease. Defining possible laboratory tests that can detect genetic disorders and evaluating the most cost-efficient and accurate laboratory tests that could be introduced to the national pre-marital screening programme. Optimizing a new panel of tests to introduce to the current pre-marital screening programme considering the panel's cost-efficiency, accuracy, reliability, and applicability. We are expecting to develop an extended version of the current national pre-marital screening programme, which involves additional tests that detect genetic disorders associated with the development of hereditary diseases among Saudis, such findings will help to achieve multiple goals of the Saudi Vision 2030. Managing to extend the current national pre-marital screening programme to involve tests for extra genetic disorders could aid to reduce the risk of developing hereditary diseases, thus, promote the productivity of individuals and efficient use of healthcare system resources.
